# SKILL CARD: Campaign Strategy & Planning
**Load this when:** Setting up, reviewing, or advising on a campaign.

---

## Campaign Objectives
A campaign should always have one primary goal. Identify it first before evaluating anything else.

| Objective | What to measure |
|---|---|
| Brand Awareness | Reach, Impressions, CPM |
| Engagement | Likes, Comments, Shares, Saves, ER |
| Conversions | Promo code redemptions, link clicks, CPA |
| Community Growth | New followers, profile visits |

---

## Influencer Tiers
> Look for follower count in: Campaign Tracker or SOW tab → `Followers`, `Audience Size`

| Tier | Follower Range | Best used for |
|---|---|---|
| Nano | 1K–10K | Hyper-niche, high trust, low cost |
| Micro | 10K–100K | Niche audiences, strong engagement |
| Macro | 100K–1M | Broad reach with some niche relevance |
| Mega/Celebrity | 1M+ | Mass awareness, high cost |

---

## Campaign Checklist
Before launching or reviewing a campaign, confirm:
- [ ] Campaign objective is defined
- [ ] Target audience is documented
- [ ] Influencer tier matches objective and budget
- [ ] KPIs are set with specific numeric targets
- [ ] Content brief has been sent and approved
- [ ] Posting dates are confirmed
- [ ] Tracking links or promo codes are in place
- [ ] FTC disclosure requirements communicated to creator
- [ ] Contract is signed *(check Contracts tab)*
- [ ] Budget is allocated *(check Dashboard tab)*

## Budget Allocation Guide
> Look for this in: Dashboard tab → `Budget`, `Split`, `Allocated`, `Spent`, `Remaining`

- **Creator fees** — typically 60–70% of total campaign budget
- **Paid amplification** (boosting posts) — typically 20–30%
- **Tools & tracking** — typically 5–10%

If the budget split in the Dashboard tab differs from this, flag it for review.
