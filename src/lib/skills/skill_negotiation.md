# Bloom Skill: Influencer Deal Negotiation

## NEGOTIATION PHILOSOPHY

Bloom approaches every creator negotiation as a relationship investment, not a transaction. The goal is always a win-win where both parties feel respected and the creator is motivated to deliver exceptional content.

**Core principles:**
- **Data-driven**: Every number we propose is anchored to actual performance data, benchmarks, and campaign history
- **Relationship-aware**: A $1,000 rate concession to a top performer who consistently delivers 8% engagement is a better investment than saving $2,000 on someone who underperforms
- **Win-win focused**: We never lowball so hard that a creator resents the deal or phones in the work
- **Strategic flexibility**: Know when to push (budget constraints, first-time creator, excessive asks) and when to fold (strategic creator, strong past performance, healthy relationship)
- **Psychology-informed**: Frame numbers in packages, not isolated line items. A "3-post + reel deal at $8,000" lands differently than "$2,500 per post"

Never open a negotiation by revealing your full budget. Always ask for their rate card first.

## READING SOW HISTORY

Before negotiating with any creator, pull up their SOW tab and extract maximum intelligence.

**Previous rates paid to this creator:**
- Look for columns labeled: "Fee", "Rate", "Compensation", "Payout", "Total Value", "Creator Payout"
- Note the platform (Instagram rates != TikTok rates)
- Track rate trajectory: Has their rate increased? By how much? When?
- If they've been with us 3+ times, they've earned the right to incremental rate increases (typically 10-15% per engagement)

**Deliverables history:**
- What content types have they provided? (Feed posts, Stories, Reels, TikToks, UGC, long-form video)
- Quantity: Did they do 1 post or 12? Single platform or multi-platform?
- Quality indicators in notes: "Exceptional engagement", "Missed deadline", "Requested 5 revision rounds"
- Scope creep patterns: Did they ask for add-ons mid-campaign?

**Contract terms and patterns:**
- **Exclusivity**: What windows have we negotiated before? (e.g., 30-day exclusivity, 60-day if they're a repeat partner)
- **Usage rights**: Duration (3 months, 6 months, perpetual?) and channels (Instagram feed only, all paid channels, organic only)
- **Kill fees**: Do we have a standing kill fee? (Should be 25-50% of contract value)
- **Revision rounds**: What's the historical standard? (Typically 1-2 rounds included, more = paid)
- **Payment terms**: Net-30? Upfront? Milestone-based? Have we accommodated expedited payment before?

**Relationship history:**
- How many campaigns have we done together? (1x = first-time leverage; 3+ = loyalty discount potential)
- Any issues in the past? (Late deliveries, quality concerns, scope creep) — Flag for negotiation leverage or risk mitigation
- Peak engagement rate? (Use this as justification for rate increase or to anchor negotiations)

## FIRST-TIME vs REPEAT CREATOR STRATEGY

### **First-Time Creator Approach**

You have more negotiation leverage with first-time creators because they don't have performance data with your brand yet.

- **Rate anchoring**: Start 10-15% below their ask to create room for negotiation without insulting them
- **Package value**: Reframe the deal as a complete package: "We're looking at a 3-post + 1 reel deal with 3-month usage rights across paid channels, plus we'll promote your account in our brand story"
- **Fast payment sweetener**: Offer net-7 instead of net-30 to justify a lower base rate ("We can offer $X if we can process payment net-7")
- **Proof of performance**: Ask for their best-performing content as a portfolio piece; use this as justification for any rate concessions they want
- **Kill fee**: Always include a 25-50% kill fee with first-time creators (your risk is higher until they prove they deliver)
- **Contingency clauses**: Include reasonable performance expectations or revision caps (2 rounds included, additional rounds $250/each)

**Language**: "We love your aesthetic and think your audience aligns perfectly with our brand. For first-time partnerships, we typically structure deals to include [X deliverables] at [Y budget]. This gives us room to establish a strong working relationship and potentially grow it going forward."

### **Repeat Creator Approach**

You have less negotiation leverage with repeat creators—they've proven themselves and know their value. Invest in loyalty here.

- **Leverage past performance**: "Your last campaign averaged 7.2% engagement, which is 40% above platform average. We want to incentivize another run with the same energy"
- **Rate increase**: Offer 10-15% bump from their last rate as a loyalty increase (it's cheaper than finding a new creator)
- **Streamlined process**: Reduce revision rounds (you know they deliver quality) and kill fees (you trust them)
- **Loyalty perks**:
  - Priority on new campaigns
  - Guaranteed minimum annual spend if you do 3+ campaigns/year
  - First access to exclusive brand partnerships
  - Performance bonus structure for repeat work
- **Reference what worked**: "Your carousel posts outperformed Reels 2:1 last time. Can we focus the deliverables there?"
- **Flex on terms**: If they request faster payment, multi-platform rights, or extended usage, consider it—retention is worth more than $2K

**Language**: "We've loved working with you on [X campaigns]. Your audience consistently engages at [Y rate], and we want to deepen this partnership. For repeat work, we're able to offer [Z rate] plus [loyalty perk]. What would make this deal work for you?"

### **If We've Had Issues**

Address past problems directly and use them as negotiation leverage.

- **Late deliveries**: "In the past, we've had timing challenges. For this campaign, we need to lock in firm deadlines. We can offer [rate] if we can build in a 48-hour submission buffer"
- **Quality concerns**: Lower the rate and tighten scope: "We want to work with you again, but we need to adjust expectations. For this campaign, let's define deliverables very clearly, and we can keep the rate at [X]"
- **Scope creep**: Include strict revision caps: "We'll include 1 round of revisions. Additional rounds are $[X] each"
- **Consider walking**: If past issues are significant, weigh whether re-engagement is worth the risk. Sometimes the cheapest deal is not working with someone again.

## RATE BENCHMARKS BY TIER

Use these as anchors when negotiating. Actual rates vary based on engagement, audience quality, and campaign complexity, but these are realistic starting points.

### **Nano Influencers (1K-10K followers)**
- **Instagram Feed Post**: $50-$300
- **Instagram Reel**: $150-$800
- **TikTok Video**: $75-$400
- **UGC/B-roll**: $100-$400
- **Usage rights add-on**: Minimal ($0-$100 for organic-only content)
- **Typical deal structure**: 1-2 posts, minimal usage rights, net-30 payment

### **Micro Influencers (10K-100K followers)**
- **Instagram Feed Post**: $300-$2,000
- **Instagram Reel**: $500-$3,000
- **TikTok Video**: $350-$1,500
- **YouTube Short**: $400-$2,000
- **Usage rights add-on**: $200-$500 for paid channel rights
- **Typical deal structure**: 3-4 content pieces, 3-month usage rights, standard exclusivity window

### **Mid-Tier Influencers (100K-500K followers)**
- **Instagram Feed Post**: $2,000-$10,000
- **Instagram Reel**: $3,000-$15,000
- **TikTok Video**: $1,500-$7,000
- **YouTube Integration (sponsored video)**: $5,000-$25,000
- **Usage rights add-on**: $500-$2,000
- **Typical deal structure**: 4-6 pieces of content, 6-month usage rights, may request exclusivity window

### **Macro Influencers (500K-1M followers)**
- **Instagram Feed Post**: $10,000-$30,000
- **Instagram Reel**: $12,000-$40,000
- **TikTok Video**: $5,000-$25,000
- **YouTube Integration**: $20,000-$75,000
- **Usage rights add-on**: $2,000-$5,000
- **Typical deal structure**: Higher-touch, may negotiate on package, strong exclusivity leverage

### **Mega Influencers (1M+ followers)**
- **Instagram Feed Post**: $30,000-$100,000+
- **Instagram Reel**: $40,000-$150,000+
- **TikTok Video**: $20,000-$75,000+
- **YouTube Integration**: $50,000-$250,000+
- **Usage rights**: Often negotiated separately at premium rates
- **Typical deal structure**: Extensive negotiation, may include equity/revenue share, long-term partnerships

### **Platform-Specific Adjustments**

- **TikTok rates**: Typically 30-50% lower than Instagram for the same follower count (lower cost per view, younger audience, different monetization)
- **YouTube integrations**: Anchor rates to 1-3% of subscriber count as a rough starting point (e.g., 500K subscriber = $5,000-$15,000 for integration video)
- **LinkedIn**: 20-30% premium vs Instagram for same follower count (B2B content, professional audience)
- **Podcast guest spots**: $1,000-$5,000 depending on audience size and production quality

### **Engagement Rate Multiplier**

If engagement is significantly above benchmarks, add 20-30% to rates. If below, subtract 15-25%.

## NEGOTIATION TACTICS

### **The Bundle Swap**
Offer a lower per-post rate in exchange for more deliverables. This makes your budget go further and gives the creator volume.

**How it works**: "We can't hit your $4,000/post ask, but what if we structured this as 4 posts + 1 reel at $12,000 total?" That's $2,400/post equivalent, but feels better as a package.

**When to use**: When a creator's per-unit rate is above your budget, but you have room for more deliverables. Works especially well with repeat creators who have consistent quality.

### **The Usage Rights Add-On**
Separate organic payout from paid usage rights. This makes the base rate look lower while justifying a premium for rights.

**How it works**: "We can offer $5,000 for the organic posts. If we also license these for paid channels, we'd add $2,000 to the contract."

**Why it works**: Creators feel they're being paid fairly for organic work, plus bonus for rights. You lock in usage without overpaying. Usage rights are almost always a legitimate add-on, not a discount tactic.

**When to use**: Every deal where you need paid usage rights. Always separate the conversation.

### **The Performance Clause**
Offer a base rate with an upside bonus tied to specific metrics. This aligns incentives and justifies a lower base offer.

**How it works**: "$2,500 base + $500 bonus if the post averages 5% engagement or higher" or "$3,000 base + $2,000 bonus if we hit 100K views in the first week."

**When to use**:
- Creator rates are above budget, but you want their talent
- You're testing a new creator and want to see performance before committing to higher rates
- Creator has inconsistent historical performance

**Risk**: Only use if metrics are realistic and transparent. If they can't hit them, the bonus becomes resentment, not incentive.

### **The Fast Payment Offer**
Promise accelerated payment (net-7 instead of net-30) to justify a lower base rate.

**How it works**: "We can offer $4,500 if we process payment net-7. Our standard net-30 rate would be $5,000."

**Why it works**: Creators value cash flow. A guaranteed net-7 payment often justifies a 10% discount because they can reinvest faster.

**When to use**: When you have cashflow flexibility and budget constraints. Especially effective with creators who are trying to fund other projects.

### **The Exclusivity Trade-Off**
Offer shorter exclusivity windows to justify lower rates, or vice versa.

**How it works**: "We can offer $6,000 with a 30-day exclusivity window, or $8,000 with 60-day exclusivity if you want more brand protection."

**When to use**: When a creator is pushing on rate but you need exclusivity. Flip it: make the longer exclusivity the premium option.

**Note**: Always define exactly what exclusivity means (competing brands, product categories, timeframe, platforms).

### **The Rate Card Ask**
ALWAYS ask for the creator's rate card before revealing your budget. This is your most important negotiation principle.

**How it works**: "Before we dive into numbers, can you share your rate card? What are you currently charging for [X deliverables]?"

**Why it works**:
- You learn their anchor (what they think they're worth)
- They may have already priced below what you budgeted
- You avoid anchoring high or low first
- You gather benchmark data for future negotiations

**What NOT to do**: Never, ever reveal your budget first. It's the fastest way to lose negotiation leverage.

## RED FLAGS IN NEGOTIATIONS

Pause and escalate these situations. They often signal problems ahead.

### **Creator asks for full payment upfront (especially first-time)**
- Red flag: High fraud risk, indicates cash flow desperation, or unfamiliar with professional contracts
- What to do: Offer milestone-based payment instead (50% upon contract sign, 50% upon delivery) or net-7 as a compromise
- If they insist: Walk. This signals they don't understand professional norms or have trust issues

### **Vague deliverables ("a few posts," "some content," "let's see what happens")**
- Red flag: Scope creep incoming. Creator will deliver minimum viable content
- What to do: ALWAYS define exactly: "2 carousel posts + 1 Reel = 3 deliverables. Each post is minimum 5 images, each Reel is 15-30 seconds"
- Include it in writing in the contract

### **No kill fee clause discussed**
- Red flag: Creator may not understand that brands reserve the right to cancel, or they're testing you
- What to do: Always propose a kill fee (25-50% of contract value). Frame it as standard industry practice
- This protects you from scope creep and last-minute cancellations

### **Usage rights not defined**
- Red flag: Becomes a fight later. Creator may think "organic only," you think "all channels, 6 months"
- What to do: Always specify in writing: "Usage rights: Organic Instagram and TikTok, 6 months from publication, [Brand] paid channels permitted with $2,000 add-on"
- Vague rights = future disputes = relationship damage

### **Rates significantly above benchmarks without strong justification**
- Red flag: Creator is inflating value, or they're testing your budget ceiling
- What to do: Ask directly: "Your rate is 40% above benchmarks for your tier. Can you share engagement data or past performance to justify it?"
- If they can't, negotiate down. If they can, it's a fair ask and recalibrate your budget

### **Creator has multiple missed deadlines or quality issues in SOW history**
- Red flag: Pattern, not fluke. They'll do it again
- What to do: Renegotiate down 20-30% or walk. The cost of managing delays exceeds the rate savings

### **Exclusivity requests that don't make sense for category**
- Red flag: Creator may try to lock out reasonable competing brands
- What to do: Define narrowly: "Cannot work with direct competitors in [specific product category] for 30 days post-publication only" instead of blanket 6-month exclusivity

## WRITING NEGOTIATION EMAILS

When Bloom helps draft negotiation emails, they should be professional, warm, and direct. Never apologetic. Always lead with relationship value, then anchor numbers to data.

### **Structure of a strong negotiation email:**

1. **Subject line**: Clear and specific
   - Good: "Partnership Proposal: [Creator Handle] + [Brand]"
   - Bad: "Budget for collab" or "Need your rate"

2. **Opener**: Always acknowledge their work and brand fit first
   - "We've been following your content for a while and love how your audience engages with lifestyle + wellness content. [Specific observation about their work]."
   - This disarms any defensiveness about the negotiation to come

3. **Campaign context**: Brief background
   - "We're launching a 3-month campaign focused on [campaign goal]. We think your audience would be a perfect fit because [reason]."

4. **Rate negotiation**: Lead with data, not just numbers
   - "Based on our current budget for this campaign, we're working with a range of $X-$Y for [specific deliverables]. We arrived at this based on [engagement benchmarks, platform averages, past performance data]."
   - This frames the number as research-backed, not arbitrary

5. **Package details**: Be extremely specific
   - Not: "a few posts and some reels"
   - Yes: "We're looking for 3 carousel posts (5+ images each) + 1 Reel (20-30 seconds) + 1 UGC video (60 seconds)"

6. **Counter-offer language**: Always include a reason
   - "If your rate is higher, we'd love to understand what justifies it. Is it based on current engagement benchmarks, or a planned expansion of your audience?"
   - OR: "We're slightly below your ask because this is a first-time partnership and we're taking on production risk. Once we see performance, we'd love to increase for round two."

7. **Package reframe**: If negotiating down, reframe as a package
   - "What if we structured this as [X deliverables] at [Y total]? This gives us room to grow the partnership long-term."

8. **Loyalty/relationship language** (if repeat creator)
   - "We've loved the engagement on your past campaigns (averaging 6.8% vs. platform 3.2%). We want to invest in more of that, and we're working with [rate] for this round."

9. **Next steps and timeline**: Always specific
   - Not: "Let us know what you think"
   - Yes: "We're looking to lock in creator commitments by Friday. Can we jump on a quick call tomorrow to finalize details?"

10. **Tone guardrails**:
    - Professional but warm (not corporate stiff)
    - Direct about budget (not coy or evasive)
    - Never apologetic: "We're unable to meet your ask" ❌ vs. "Based on benchmarks, our range is $X-$Y" ✅
    - Always future-focused: "This is the starting point for a long-term partnership"

## QUICK REFERENCE: NEGOTIATION DECISION TREE

**Is this a first-time creator?**
- YES → Start 10-15% below ask, use package value, offer fast payment sweetener, include kill fee
- NO → Reference past performance, offer 10-15% loyalty increase, streamline process

**Is their rate above benchmarks?**
- YES → Ask for justification data, offer performance clause, or walk
- NO → You have room to negotiate. Push slightly or accept and invest in relationship

**Do we need usage rights?**
- YES → Always separate from organic payout. Frame as $[base] + $[rights add-on]
- NO → Simplify deal, make it about organic content only

**Is our budget tight?**
- YES → Use Bundle Swap (more deliverables, lower per-unit rate) or Performance Clause
- NO → Negotiate fair market rate, focus on relationship value

**Have we had issues with this creator before?**
- YES → Address directly, negotiate lower rate or reconsider partnership entirely
- NO → Standard negotiation process, lean into relationship

## FINAL PRINCIPLE

The best negotiation is one where both parties walk away feeling they won. Never optimize for the lowest rate at the cost of resentment or underdelivery. A creator earning fair value for fair work will go the extra mile. A creator who feels lowballed will send you their minimum viable content.

We negotiate hard on numbers, but soft on relationship.
